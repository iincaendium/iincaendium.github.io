Brush stroke on the soul
Giving it color
My friends and I as a whole
In a study room
During the quiet hours
Light stroke on the heart
Splitting it into the halves
Now, I hardly can feel it
I’m slowly forgetting all my past
And switching to a vortex of unsettled feelings
For real, I think, my university years should be recorded and stored in a file
Because, somehow, my greatly uneventful life
Started running a multitude of extra miles
And from all the anxiety I go outside
Where stormy wind cools my inner system
Sometimes I feel like an old buzzing computer
With an ancient Windows Vista
It’s time to change the thermal paste
But anyways, why bother?
I was completely ok with inner self
I never liked solving puzzles
Now I’m a sculptor
Shaping my priorities
Professionally dealing with groceries
And making a final project for arts and culture
No matter what, I’m convincing myself
I’ll find a suiting role
And fix shortness of breath
And with full lungs of air
On some kind of fundament
Maybe from cement, maybe from sticks and foliage
The halves of the heart will reunite
LCC chronicle will be completed
My feet finally will feel the ground
And I will start my path on it.
